# GitHub Growth & Stars Scaling Strategy

This document details the scaling targets and acquisition strategies to grow the **Career-Agents** repository metrics.

---

## 📈 Star Milestones & Targets

We map our marketing and community initiatives across 5 chronological star milestones:

### 1. Target: 100 Stars (Onboarding)
- **Primary Channels:** Personal LinkedIn posts, Reddit (r/python, r/cscareerquestions), and initial developer newsletters.
- **Key Focus:** Proving validation checks, fixing setup friction, and getting early contributor feedback.

### 2. Target: 500 Stars (Traction)
- **Primary Channels:** Developer communities (Dev.to, Hashnode publications), targeted HackerNews submissions, and Reddit tool showcases.
- **Key Focus:** Launching the multi-tool converters and CLI installer.

### 3. Target: 1,000 Stars (Growth)
- **Primary Channels:** Product Hunt launch, LinkedIn organic posts, and partnerships with student developer clubs.
- **Key Focus:** Landing the first 65+ agents and establishing standardized schemas.

### 4. Target: 5,000 Stars (Platform Validation)
- **Primary Channels:** Technical influencer alignments, college university placement cell alignments, and DevRel conference workshops.
- **Key Focus:** Reaching 100+ agents, launching Tauri Desktop app container, and starting the Community Marketplace.

### 5. Target: 10,000 Stars (OS Ecosystem)
- **Primary Channels:** Multi-tool integrations promotions (Cursor Rules registry, Claude Code subagents libraries, etc.), major corporate sponsorships, and university certification programs.
- **Key Focus:** Building a global Career Operating System.

---

## 📢 Multi-Channel Sourcing

- **SEO & Content:** Write highly searchable articles targeting specific career preparation keywords (e.g. "FAANG System Design preparation roadmap", "ATS resume formatting checklist").
- **Product Hunt:** Structured launch of the Career OS Desktop App and Marketplace.
- **Reddit & HackerNews:** Share open-source technical developer platforms, avoiding generic prompt-library terminology.
- **Developer Communities:** Host regular tutorials and sample codes showcasing automated installation CLI configurations.
