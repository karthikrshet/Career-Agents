# Remote Job Hunt

## Objective
Build a remote-first job search workflow that prioritizes distributed teams, timezone fit, and asynchronous hiring channels.

## Target audience
- Developers who want fully remote or hybrid-remote positions.
- Candidates seeking flexible schedules, digital nomad roles, or globally distributed teams.
- Anyone improving their remote hiring visibility and outreach.

## Recommended agents
- `career/job-search-strategist.md`
- `career/recruiter-outreach-specialist.md`
- `career/linkedin-growth-advisor.md`
- `career/resume-strategist.md`
- `career/technical-interview-coach.md`

## Step-by-step process
1. Define the remote profile you want: time zone preference, async culture, and team size.
2. Use `job-search-strategist` to map remote-friendly companies, marketplaces, and referral sources.
3. Optimize your resume for remote roles with `resume-strategist`, showcasing distributed work and communication skills.
4. Use `linkedin-growth-advisor` to clarify your remote availability and location flexibility.
5. Develop a recruiter outreach plan with `recruiter-outreach-specialist` that highlights remote strengths.
6. Prepare for remote interview formats and asynchronous coding challenges with `technical-interview-coach`.
7. Track each application, recruiter response, and timezone alignment.

## Weekly execution plan
Week 1: Define remote target profiles and complete remote resume/profile optimization.
Week 2: Launch targeted remote applications and outreach to remote-friendly hiring teams.
Week 3: Conduct mock remote interview exercises and asynchronous test runs.
Week 4: Review offers, verify timezone/culture fit, and prepare negotiation strategy.

## Success metrics
- Number of remote-specific applications submitted.
- Response rate from remote-friendly recruiters.
- Interview invitations in the preferred timezone window.
- Quality of remote interview feedback.
- Alignment score for remote working conditions.

## Common mistakes
- Applying to remote roles without clarifying preferred working hours.
- Submitting a generic resume that does not mention distributed work skills.
- Underestimating the importance of communication and documentation in remote roles.
- Failing to verify timezone and overlap expectations early.

## Expected outcomes
- A remote-focused job search funnel.
- A recruiter-ready remote profile and outreach sequence.
- Stronger positioning for distributed engineering roles.
- Better odds of landing a remote offer that fits your schedule and lifestyle.
