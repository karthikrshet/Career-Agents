# Internship Hunt

## Objective
Build a repeatable internship pursuit system that turns resumes, outreach, and application tracking into a predictable pipeline.

## Target audience
- Undergraduate students seeking first or next internship.
- Early-career candidates preparing for summer, winter, or sponsored internship cycles.
- Anyone targeting campus placement, industry internships, or apprenticeship roles.

## Recommended agents
- `career/internship-application-strategist.md`
- `career/resume-strategist.md`
- `career/ats-resume-reviewer.md`
- `career/recruiter-outreach-specialist.md`
- `career/linkedin-growth-advisor.md`
- `career/placement-coach.md`

## Step-by-step process
1. Audit your current priorities: target industries, role types, locations, learning goals, and availability.
2. Build an internship-ready story with `resume-strategist` and align it to at least 3 target role families.
3. Run the draft resume through `ats-resume-reviewer` and implement the formatting, keyword, and section improvements.
4. Document a target list of companies, programs, and recruiters with `placement-coach` and `job-search-strategist` thinking.
5. Use `linkedin-growth-advisor` to optimize your LinkedIn profile and recruiter visibility for internship keywords.
6. Draft tailored outreach messages with `recruiter-outreach-specialist` for campus recruiters, alumni connections, and hiring managers.
7. Apply systematically, track every submission, and review recruiters’ feedback weekly.
8. Use `placement-coach` to prepare follow-ups, next steps, and backup application tracks.

## Weekly execution plan
Week 1: Define goals, refine resume, and complete the first application batch.
Week 2: Launch LinkedIn outreach, expand the target list, and iterate on messaging.
Week 3: Optimize applications using feedback, add second-tier companies, and prepare for any screening calls.
Week 4: Review response patterns, prioritize interviews, and use placement-coach to close the top internship opportunities.

## Success metrics
- Number of internship applications submitted on schedule.
- Response rate from recruiters or hiring teams.
- Number of interviews secured within 4 weeks.
- Improvement in resume visibility score and LinkedIn engagement.
- Clarity on the most promising internship tracks.

## Common mistakes
- Sending the same application to every role without specialization.
- Ignoring ATS signals and using a resume that is too decorative or poorly structured.
- Treating outreach as one-off messages instead of a follow-up sequence.
- Applying only to “dream companies” and not building a reliable fallback pipeline.

## Expected outcomes
- A polished, internship-ready application package.
- An organized outreach and application tracker.
- A shortlist of interviews and opportunities to pursue.
- Clear next steps for converting screening calls into offers.
