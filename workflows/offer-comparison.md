# Offer Comparison

## Objective
Create a systematic offer comparison process that makes choosing between multiple job opportunities clear and defensible.

## Target audience
- Candidates evaluating two or more offers.
- Job seekers who want to compare compensation, growth, culture, and fit objectively.
- Anyone who needs to avoid decision paralysis and select the best long-term option.

## Recommended agents
- `career/offer-evaluation-advisor.md`
- `career/salary-negotiation-coach.md`
- `career/hr-interview-coach.md`
- `career/placement-coach.md`

## Step-by-step process
1. Gather offer details across compensation, role scope, team, career path, and culture.
2. Use `offer-evaluation-advisor` to score each offer on key dimensions.
3. Create an offer comparison matrix with `placement-coach` or a spreadsheet.
4. Factor in timing, risk, future growth, and personal priorities.
5. Use `salary-negotiation-coach` to understand which offer has room for improvement.
6. Review each offer against your long-term career goals.

## Weekly execution plan
Week 1: Collect all offer data and build the initial comparison matrix.
Week 2: Score offers on compensation, role fit, development, and culture.
Week 3: Evaluate benefits, flexibility, and growth potential.
Week 4: Finalize the decision with objective criteria and a personal recommendation.

## Success metrics
- Clarity in the relative strengths and weaknesses of each offer.
- Objective score for compensation, growth, and fit.
- Confidence in the decision based on prioritized criteria.
- Ability to explain the choice to mentors or decision partners.
- Reduced decision fatigue and better long-term alignment.

## Common mistakes
- Choosing only by headline salary rather than total value.
- Neglecting growth potential, role scope, or culture fit.
- Forgetting to evaluate timeline and future earning potential.
- Letting bias or external pressure override your own priorities.

## Expected outcomes
- A clear offer comparison report.
- A defensible decision framework.
- Better alignment between the chosen offer and your long-term path.
- A documented rationale for accepting or declining offers.
