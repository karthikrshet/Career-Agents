# Salary Negotiation

## Objective
Build a negotiation-ready compensation strategy that turns offers into confident decisions and better outcomes.

## Target audience
- Candidates receiving offers for internships, entry-level, or mid-career roles.
- Anyone who needs to negotiate salary, equity, benefits, or counteroffers.
- Job seekers who want to avoid leaving money or opportunity on the table.

## Recommended agents
- `career/salary-negotiation-coach.md`
- `career/offer-evaluation-advisor.md`
- `career/hr-interview-coach.md`
- `career/recruiter-outreach-specialist.md`

## Step-by-step process
1. Collect all offer details, including base salary, bonus, equity, benefits, and timeline.
2. Use `offer-evaluation-advisor` to compare total compensation and fit across offers.
3. Run negotiation scenarios with `salary-negotiation-coach` to identify leverage and fallback positions.
4. Align your ask with role expectations, market benchmarks, and company stage.
5. Prepare the negotiation message or conversation plan.
6. Use `hr-interview-coach` to rehearse asking for the revised offer and handling follow-up questions.

## Weekly execution plan
Week 1: Gather offer details and complete the initial evaluation.
Week 2: Build the negotiation strategy and prepare ask scripts.
Week 3: Execute the negotiation conversation and review feedback.
Week 4: Finalize the agreed offer terms and close the decision process.

## Success metrics
- Gain in total compensation versus the original offer.
- Quality of the negotiation ask and rationale.
- Clarity of fallback positions and walk-away criteria.
- Candidate confidence in the negotiation conversation.
- Speed of agreement and clarity of final terms.

## Common mistakes
- Negotiating without a clear benchmark or rationale.
- Asking only for salary and ignoring total compensation.
- Treating compensation as a one-time ask rather than a conversation.
- Accepting terms before fully evaluating option value and fit.

## Expected outcomes
- A clear pay strategy and ask plan.
- A stronger, market-aligned compensation package.
- Better alignment between offer terms and personal priorities.
- Confidence in closing the negotiation with a professional tone.
